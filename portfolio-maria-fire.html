Maria Fire

Maria Fire

The Maria Fire, an almost 10,000-acre wildfire, broke out between Somis and Santa Paula on Halloween evening, 10/31/2019. In situations that last more than a day or so, resources can quickly deplete. As such, all Ventura County Search & Rescue teams are always ready to assist, and often called when natural disasters strike. For the Maria fire, our role was to issue evacuations or encourage those who had previously chosen to remain in their homes to leave if they were in imminent danger.

Info
Dropping Phos-Chek

Multiple DC-10s and other water tankers were making the trip back and forth between San Bernardino and the Maria fire to dump Phos-Chek and drastically slow the rate at which the fire could spread.

Info
Shoveling Dirt

It's nearly impossible for firefighters to cover the entire perimeter of the fire to put it out. As such, when we weren't evacuating people, we were doing everything we could to help - including shoveling dirt on small flames to stop the spread.

Info
Burning Property

Unfortunately, sometimes fire spreads to peoples' property before it can be put out. In this picture, you can see black smoke coming from a burning structure while firefighters on the other side are quickly working to extinguish it.

Info
Helicopters

Just as many airplanes were dropping Phos-Chek to slow the fire, many helicopters were dropping water to extinguish it. In wildland, there are also many places fire trucks can't quite reach so helicopters are an invaluable resource.

Info
Phos-Chek Line

This photo shows what an excellent retardant Phos-Chek can be. The fire is slowly smoldering out where it ran into the Phos-Chek, effectively stopping the fire from spreading.
