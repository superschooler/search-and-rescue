Hungry Valley

Hungry Valley and it's surrounding areas are where a large portion of our call-outs take place. As the Fillmore Search and Rescue team, our area of coverage is generally the northeast segment of the county, and Hungry Valley is the tip of this area with many activities, from mountain biking and hiking to cross-country skiing and off-roading. The fastest method of travel (besides the occasional helicopter ride) is to drive out of Ventura County to Valencia, then north on the 5 for about half an hour. Although it's a lengthy drive, it's a great area to train and patrol and coverage is a necessity.

Off-Road Equipment

We use our off-road equipment for patrols on Memorial Day and Labor Day as well as for call-outs where hiking and motorcycle trails can give us quick access to a large area.

Info
Training

We often incorporate long and strenuous hikes into our training to keep in top physical shape. Combined with the elevation in some areas of the county as well as the heavy equipment we need to carry for rescues, our training and call-outs can be challenging both physically and mentally.

Info
Helicopter Training

Occasionally, we're fortunate enough to have a helicopter available to train with us. This training gives us valuable knowledge for when a call-out requires we be airlifted into the backcountry to search for or rescue a victim.

Info
Heavy Packs

Our packs can easily weigh as much as 40 or 50 pounds when loaded with our ropes, harnesses, caribeaners, and other rescue equipment, water, other essentials, and any medical or systems gear we need to carry into our destination.

Info
Hiking & Camping

The views atop the peaks in Hungry Valley can be breathtaking. We'll often head up the night before our training and camp off the trail to experience nature.
