October Training

This month's training consisted of two parts. First, we practiced improvizing and completing a rescue with only our team members' equipment - no systems ropes, EMT equipment, or stokes basket. Luckily, several of our members carry gear above and beyond what's required by the team. We were able to quickly set up a very efficient raising and lowering system to raise our whole team, two at a time, up a waterfall, then practice lowering our EMTs down the waterfall to the "victim" and raising them back up together. To finish, we all rappelled down the waterfall and completed the trek out.

The second portion of our training took place at the Air Unit, where one of our members who works at the County's EMS Agency led a Stop the Bleed training, where we practiced using tourniquets, applying bandages, and packing wounds. The majority of our team carries a tourniquet on them at all times when in the field - you never know when it could save a life.
